About

a

I'm Vishnu Varatharajan, a Political Science PhD student at the Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies, Geneva, and a Coding & Analysis Intern at Global Survivors Fund.

I study Ethnocentrism and Majoritarian politics, using qualitative methods and cognitive mapping approach. My field of interest is Cognitive Political Psychology, and I'm looking at how people conflate their religious identity and territorial attachment, leading to a majoritarian possessiveness towards the land, and in worst cases, normalizing the exclusionary policies of the state. My current region of focus is India and Ireland. I intend to conduct ethnographic interviews, tracing the cognitive processes involved in people’s beliefs on religion and nationhood, and presenting the results in the form of difference and possibilities in cognitive maps.
